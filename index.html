Reconsidering myth as a cultural technology for environmental preservation.

I. The Modern Divide

In the modern Western world, myth is often understood as the opposite of truth.

It is associated with fiction, imagination, and stories that belong to a distant and less rational past. Scientific reasoning has become the dominant framework for understanding reality.

Yet this way of thinking represents a relatively recent shift. For most of human existence, myth was one of the primary ways societies preserved essential information about survival.

Before the rise of scientific rationalism, many cultures understood reality through interconnected spiritual and ecological systems.

Myth served as a bridge between observation and meaning, encoding environmental experience into narratives.
